Brief Profile

Understand what is BGVS on ground

Vermi Compost Demonstration Programme – 2011 to 2022

Focus:

Low-cost inputs, promoting sustainable agriculture, including organic agriculture.

Target Group:

  • Rainfed areas
  • SC/ST families
  • Small and marginal farmers
  • Areas growing crops with special characteristics/geographical indications

Intervention:

  • End of March 2011, in Jhunjhunu district, 8 demonstrations were organized in Jhunjhunu and Nawalgarh block.
  • A Kisan Gosti was organized at Bai village to train farmers on vermiculture and Nadep compost.
  • In Sikar district, 15 village-level farmer gostis were organized for vermicompost.
  • In 7 villages, vermicompost demonstration units were installed on the farms of 7 farmers. Farmers were also trained on crop-wise dosage for application of vermicompost.
  • By the end of 2011, 565 farmers benefited from the programme.

Farmers Interest Group Formation Programme – 2012

Focus:

Enhancing profits from agriculture through sustainable agriculture, including organic agriculture and an integrated agricultural approach through Capacity Building of farmers.

Target Group:

  • Rainfed areas
  • SC/ST families
  • Small and marginal farmers
  • Areas growing crops with special characteristics/geographical indications

Intervention:

  • In Jhunjhunu district, BGVS formed Farmers Interest Groups (FIGs) in Nawalgarh block in 2011. In Nawalgarh block, 11 villages were covered, and 20 FIGs were formulated, benefiting 155 farmers. In Nawalgarh block, 19 villages were covered, and 28 FIGs were formulated, benefiting 177 farmers. In Nawalgarh block, 5 villages were covered, and 9 FIGs were formulated, benefiting 108 farmers.
  • In 6 villages, 13 Farmer Interest Groups were formulated, benefiting 187 farmers in Sikar district.
  • In Sikar district, 12 FIGs of organic agriculture, 2 FIGs of dairying, 5 FIGs of horticulture crops, and 2 FIGs of vegetables were formulated, benefiting 210 farmers.

Women Reproductive, Health & Hygiene and Sanitation Programme – 2011 to 2022

Focus:

Mass awareness generation regarding safe motherhood, child care, and improved health hygiene and sanitation behavior among 17 villages of Jhunjhunu district, through BGVS managed by women and girl health animators.

Intervention:

  • A training programme was organized in Nawalgarh village on the immunization of children and pregnant women.
  • On October 23, 2010, a one-day training programme was organized at Jhunjhunu district headquarters.

Development of Horticulture and Pasture under Watershed Development Program

Community Served:

More than 300 farmers were provided knowledge and support for horticulture and pasture development.

Objective:

The objective was to increase the area and productivity of horticulture produce and improve personal and community pastures.

Strategies:

  • Coverage of more than 4 micro watersheds through training programs.

Main Outcomes:

  • More than 300 farmers were trained in vermiculture.
  • About 600 demonstration units of vermiculture were set up.
  • All major horticulture crops grown in the area were covered under the program, and personal and community pastures were also targeted for coverage under the program.

Evaluation Methods Employed:

  • Field Visits
  • Review Meetings

Evaluation Results:

All 250 farmers trained were actively using their training for horticulture and pasture development in their area.

Nirmal Gram Yojna under Sampoorna Swachta Karyakram

Community Served:

All communities of Gram Panchayat Mohanbari in Nawalgarh Tehsil of Jhunjhunu district.

Objective:

Building awareness and action towards a clean environment, hygienic living surroundings, and healthy people.

Strategies:

  • Mass meetings along with the Gram Sarpanch.
  • Screening of videos on health, cleanliness, and environment.

Main Outcome:

  • Villagers have begun to keep their surroundings clean.
  • A vigilance group/committee has been developed for social policing in the area to ensure that personal, environmental, and all-round health and hygiene are gradually enhanced and maintained.

Enhancing the Quality of Education

BGVS is actively engaged with local schools in the area, helping them improve their curriculum, increase enrollment (especially of girls), and increase teacher attendance. BGVS has also integrated this program with the health, hygiene, and sanitation program, ensuring that young school students and youth take an active part in conducting awareness programs for health, hygiene, and sanitation in the area. Various competitions are organized from time to time to build awareness among future citizens about their roles and responsibilities towards ensuring the hygiene and sanitation of their surroundings and ensuring their own personal health.
